## Introduction: This can be copied from the proposal.
SimpleTM (https://openreview.net/pdf?id=oANkBaVci5) is the model we’re wishing to replicate and test. They claim two main points: one being that this simpler model is as effective or more effective than more complex models including transformers; two being that stationary wavelet transformation is also effective in multivariate time series. Their model simply uses a linear projection -> wavelet transform -> geometric attention -> inverse wavelet transform -> FFN and layernorm -> MSE. First, we plan to test it on new datasets including text with tagging data, sports team performance data, and larger time series datasets like the PhysioNet 2012 ICU dataset. Furthermore, given the architectural simplicity of the model, it is possible to scrutinize the contributions of each innovative component — the transformation and attention mechanisms — to overall model performance. We aim to implement alternatives to the wavelet transform, potentially including the Fast Fourier transform (as in the authors’ ablation studies), Short-Term Fourier Transform, Hilbert–Huang transform, and a learnable Haar transform (https://arxiv.org/html/2504.08801v1). And, we would also test alternatives to the attention mechanism by replacing it with a traditional transformer, Grassman Flows, and continue testing their traditional attention ablation. It would also be beneficial to benchmark the attention-based architectures against a “classical” baseline (e.g. ARIMA).
## Challenges: What has been the hardest part of the project you've encountered so far?
It was challenging to understand the mathematical formulation of the wavelet decomposition, both as a signal preprocessing technique and as it related to the attention mechanisms. The recursive splitting of the signal into various sub-signals at different resolutions is clever. It was initially challenging to see that each convolutional layer was not operating on the same original signal, but rather on . Various hard-coded assumptions in the authors’ repository were also difficult to understand as either necessary or presumptuous (for example, the circular padding mechanism used to maintain pseudo-length). It was also difficult understanding their choices for hyperparameters because they were not really consistent across and within datasets. Thus, we weren’t really sure which hyperparameters ended up being the best ones shown in the paper and what we should use for our replication of their baseline.

We only have preliminary results that perform in line with their baseline results. We have not done any ablations or alternative experiments yet.
